Weight & Type 2 Diabetes

shipped

A sixth pet stat, weight, tracked the same way as Hunger/Tired/Drained/Sick/Miserable — 0 is best, full-scale is worst. Shown as the Fit bar in the Stats popover.

How it moves

Weight drifts up on its own, slowly — a multi-day arc rather than an hours-scale stat like Hunger. Left completely alone it crosses the overweight line in roughly 4–5 days. Feeding adds a further nudge on top, sized entirely by what was eaten. Exercising is the only way to bring it back down.

The diabetes trigger

weight > 60% → sustained 3 days (Classic) / 6 days (Casual) → diabetic = true, permanently

Dropping weight back under the line before that window elapses resets the clock to zero — no partial credit. Once triggered, though, there's no cure, only management: while diabetic and unmedicated, Sick accrues an extra penalty every tick, on top of whatever else is already pushing it up.

action

Give medication

shipped

Lives inside the existing Heal icon, as a second item alongside "Give medicine" — the same pattern Rest already uses for Sleep + Relax under one icon. No new icon slot needed.

Only selectable once the pet is diabetic; shows as a permanently disabled row before that, same treatment as "Defrost" sitting disabled outside Hibernate. Administering it resets a cooldown that doubles as a protection window: while it's running, the diabetes sick-penalty is suppressed. When it lapses, the penalty resumes right as the item becomes selectable again — the game's way of telling you it's time to re-dose.

Food choice

shipped

Feed is now a five-item choice instead of one flat action. Each food scales the same baseline hunger/weight/drained numbers by a different percentage — Apple sits at 100% across the board, so it's a drop-in match for what plain Feed always did.

One shared cooldown across all five — it's still just "feeding," whatever's on the plate.

asset

Sprite & icon coverage

shipped

Every new menu action now has matching art — the badge's sprite loader fails soft on a missing file (same behaviour as a factory-reset badge with no art at all), so a blank slot doesn't crash anything, it just looks unfinished. This pass closes that gap.

SlotWhat's thereFramesStatus
Exercise menu icondumbbell glyph, top row col 32done
Exercising animationeach pet's idle body + a dumbbell prop in whatever canvas space that pet leaves open2 × 3 petsdone
Medicating animationidle body + a pill-bottle/cross prop1 × 3 petsdone

The prop-over-static-body approach is lifted directly from how the existing Playing animation works — the pet doesn't change pose, a prop just appears in open canvas space next to it.

Verification note — art was round-trip verified byte-for-byte against its own encoder and sanity-checked as ASCII art, but the actual e-paper/simulator render hasn't been seen firsthand. Worth a look in make sim or on hardware before calling it final.

Debug cheats

shipped

A hidden menu for testing the multi-day weight/diabetes arc in seconds. Not part of normal play — no icon, no cooldowns.

Up Up Down Down Left Right Left Right Fire → opens Debug menu

Entered from the idle main screen — a mistimed press just resets the tracker, arrow presses still move the nav cursor normally while attempting it.
